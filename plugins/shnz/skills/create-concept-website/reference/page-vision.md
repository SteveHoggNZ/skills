# Section: Vision (the *why*)

The Vision section is the top-down half of the site. It is narrative, aspirational, and makes the concept feel real. After one read the reader should be able to state the one-line shift, name the pillars, and picture the destination state.

It is also the hardest section to write well. Draft it last (after Map and Roadmap) so you know what "the how" actually looks like and can avoid disconnected aspiration.

## Anti-slop discipline

Concept sites generated by AI tend to fall into recognisable visual and prose tells. The skill's defaults have been tuned against them; if you diverge from a default, check you have a concrete reason rather than a reflex.

Specifically, for this section:

- No gradient text in `<h1>`. State the concept plainly in one colour.
- No "Transform your X" / "Unlock your Y" hero phrasing.
- The `<blockquote>From X to Y</blockquote>` frame is **optional**, not mandatory. Use it when the shift really is that cleanly binary; otherwise write a sentence.
- No emoji in `.flow-badge` or `.pillar-icon`. Either leave the icon empty, or add a real SVG.
- No em-dash-per-sentence tic. Default to periods. See [voice.md](./voice.md) §"Em dashes".

## Structure (in order)

Every `<h2>` is preceded by a `<p class="eyebrow">` short uppercase label. Eyebrows orient the reader before the heading lands.

1. **Destination.** One paragraph plus a before/after contrast. Destination-state from intake verbatim where possible. Pair with a concrete before-image so the delta is visible.
2. **Big picture.** One sentence stating the shift. Use a `<blockquote>` if the shift is genuinely a from-X-to-Y binary; otherwise a normal paragraph is fine.
3. **Concept in motion.** Show how the parts connect when the concept is alive. Pick one shape:
   - **`.flow` rows** (default for simple actor → action narratives up to 5 steps): colored badges, arrows, descriptions.
   - **Mermaid `sequenceDiagram`** (use when 4+ actors hand off to each other): one diagram inside a `<figure class="diagram">`. Short participant labels, no emoji. See [diagrams.md](./diagrams.md) §Sequence.
   - **`.chain`** (use for a named sequence without separate actor / action columns; lighter than `.flow`): horizontal boxes with CSS arrows. See [diagrams.md](./diagrams.md) §`.chain`.

   If the Vision has a load-bearing "X on top of Y on top of Z" sub-concept (architecture layers, intent / context / prompting, platform tiers), add a `.stack-diagram` right after this section with its own `<p class="eyebrow">` and `<h2>`. Do not fold it into "in motion"; layered stacks and narrative flows answer different questions.
4. **Pillars.** 3 (preferred) or 4 named principles. Each a `.pillar` card with a colored heading and body. No top-border stripes. No emoji icon tiles.
5. **The moment it clicks.** A single concrete story in a `<blockquote>`, with inline `<span class="hl-*">` highlights on 2–3 dramatic terms. Highlights are what make the paragraph scannable at slide distance.
6. **Your journey.** 3–5 `.journey-card`s showing maturity levels. Each has an eyebrow ("WHERE YOU START"), a colored role name, and a body.

## Voice

- Narrative, not listy. The Vision is the one place to write paragraphs rather than bullets.
- Reuse the 1–3 chosen metaphors from intake. A metaphor that lands is worth ten abstract sentences.
- Concrete over abstract. A sentence that could describe five other concepts gets rewritten or cut.
- Confidence, not hedging.
- Second person is fine when talking about the reader. Avoid first-person plural ("we believe…").

Notes:

- Flow badge labels are short text: "Ticket opened", "Research ready", "Plan approved". No emoji. If you need an iconographic cue, use an inline SVG inside the badge.
- Pillar card colours come from the `.is-*` class. The heading inherits the colour; the top of the card is just a plain border. The eyebrow above the heading labels the category.
- The `.path` row at the end is inline wayfinding back to the tactical sections. Drop it if the Vision is already short.
- On concepts where "your journey" does not apply (e.g. one-off platform changes), replace the journey section with something the intake actually supports, or omit it. Do not fill it with a generic "adopter / advocate / expert" ladder.

## Optional forms for the "Big picture" section

The `<blockquote>From X to Y</blockquote>` shape is useful when the shift is genuinely binary. When it is not, use one of these instead:

1. Prose. A 2–3 sentence paragraph can carry a nuanced shift better than a pullquote.
2. Contrast table. Two columns: "Today's shape" / "Tomorrow's shape", 3–5 rows. Works for concepts where the shift is across multiple dimensions.
3. Short numbered list. 3 observations that together describe the change.

Pick the form that fits the concept, not the form that fits the visual reflex.

## What NOT to do

- Do not use `<span class="gradient">` around words in the hero. The class has been removed from the stylesheet.
- Do not insert emoji into `.flow-badge`, `.pillar-icon`, or `.path-emoji`. Either use a real SVG or leave it out.
- Do not replace `.flow` with a plain table. The flow gives the narrative spatial pacing that a table does not.
- Do not use the `.wave` marker here. Reserved for Roadmap.
- Do not write more than ~900 words of body text. A Vision that cannot be read in 3 minutes will not be re-read.
- Do not inline-highlight more than 3 terms per blockquote. Highlights work because they are rare.
